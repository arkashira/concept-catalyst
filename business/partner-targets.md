# partner-targets.md

## Partner Integration Roadmap for Concept-Catalyst

### 1. **Notion API**
   - **Rationale**: Notion is widely used by indie hackers for project management and documentation. Integrating with Notion allows users to seamlessly transfer generated ideas into their Notion workspace for further development.
   - **Free-tier limits**: Up to 1,000 blocks per month.
   - **Integration effort**: Medium (M)
   - **Value-add**: Enhances user workflow by allowing easy organization and tracking of ideas.

### 2. **Zapier**
   - **Rationale**: Zapier connects with over 3,000 apps, enabling users to automate workflows. By integrating with Zapier, users can automate the process of taking generated ideas and pushing them to their preferred tools (e.g., Trello, Slack).
   - **Free-tier limits**: 5 Zaps, 100 tasks per month.
   - **Integration effort**: Medium (M)
   - **Value-add**: Saves time and reduces manual effort in managing ideas across platforms.

### 3. **Airtable**
   - **Rationale**: Airtable is a flexible database tool that indie hackers can use to manage their projects. Integrating with Airtable allows users to store and categorize generated ideas in a structured format.
   - **Free-tier limits**: 1,200 records per base, 2GB attachment space.
   - **Integration effort**: Medium (M)
   - **Value-add**: Provides a customizable database for idea management.

### 4. **Figma**
   - **Rationale**: Figma is a popular design tool among creators. Integrating with Figma allows users to visualize their ideas directly in a design environment, facilitating faster prototyping.
   - **Free-tier limits**: 3 projects, unlimited collaborators.
   - **Integration effort**: Large (L)
   - **Value-add**: Bridges the gap between ideation and design, enhancing the development process.

### 5. **Stripe**
   - **Rationale**: For indie hackers looking to monetize their ideas, integrating with Stripe allows them to set up payment processing for their products directly from the concept stage.
   - **Free-tier limits**: No monthly fees, but transaction fees apply.
   - **Integration effort**: Medium (M)
   - **Value-add**: Simplifies the transition from idea to revenue generation.

### 6. **Slack**
   - **Rationale**: Many indie hackers use Slack for team communication. Integrating with Slack allows users to receive notifications and updates about their generated ideas in real-time.
   - **Free-tier limits**: 10,000 messages searchable, 1:1 video calls.
   - **Integration effort**: Small (S)
   - **Value-add**: Keeps users engaged and informed about their ideas without leaving their communication platform.

### 7. **Google Drive API**
   - **Rationale**: Google Drive is commonly used for file storage and collaboration. Integrating with Google Drive allows users to save and share generated ideas and related documents easily.
   - **Free-tier limits**: 15GB of free storage.
   - **Integration effort**: Medium (M)
   - **Value-add**: Facilitates easy sharing and collaboration on ideas.

### 8. **Trello**
   - **Rationale**: Trello is a popular project management tool among indie hackers. Integrating with Trello allows users to create boards and cards directly from generated ideas.
   - **Free-tier limits**: Unlimited cards, 10 boards per team.
   - **Integration effort**: Small (S)
   - **Value-add**: Streamlines the organization of ideas into actionable tasks.

### Conclusion
Prioritizing integrations with affiliate/revenue-share opportunities will enhance the value proposition of Concept-Catalyst while providing users with essential tools to streamline their ideation process.
